Scenario planning

An alternative way of dealing with uncertainty

Author: Goodwin, Wright

Introduction

What is Uncertainty?

  • Will I graduate?
  • Will nuclear waste storage site experience seismic activity?
  • Will the NZ economy explode?

Saddam Hussein Invades Kuwait

  • Intelligence agencies sent Bush list of predictions.
  • None had first choice that Saddam would attack.
  • Why?
    • Groupthink = group thinking becomes homogeneous.
    • Minority opinions overrun by majority.

Scenario Planning: A Better Way?

  • Build possible scenarios and plan actions.
  • Advantages:
    • Allows minority opinions to have 'airtime'.
    • Predicting future hard; instead, scenarios bound the range of possible outcomes.
    • Participants explore problem space, gaining key insights.

Method 1

The Extreme Worlds Method

Overview of Method

  • Develop two extreme worlds (scenarios)
    • Should bound all possible scenarios.
  • Eigh distinct steps
    • Can retrace steps during process.
  • Multinational NZ Dairy Monopoly
  • Revenue of $20 billion
  • 30% of world's dairy
  • Recent Botulism scare
    (bacterial contamination of milk powder)

1/8 Concern & Horizon

  • Concern: The survival and profitability of Fonterra.
  • Horizon: The next 10 years from now.

2/8 Identify Trends

  • Increasing dairy consumption (2% per year).
  • Preference to buy NZ dairy products.
  • Growing Asian market.
  • Climate change affecting production.

3/8 Identify Uncertainties

  • Consistent quality of Fonterr's products.
  • Strength of NZ dollar.
  • Ability to hold NZ monopoly.
  • Cow "fart tax" in NZ.

4/8 Impacts of Trends & Uncertainties

5/8 Create Extreme Worlds

Extreme Positive World: Blue Skies
  • Fonterra's products remain high quality.
  • The NZ dollar weakens = exporting better.
  • Fonterra continues to hold the monopoly.
  • No "fart tax" is placed on cow emissions.

5/8 Create Extreme Worlds

Extreme Negative World: Stormy Days
  • Another Botulism destroys Fonterra’s reputation.
  • The NZ dollar strengthens = more expensive product.
  • Fonterra is broken up by NZ government.
  • A high "fart tax" is passed in NZ.

6/8 Add Trends to Worlds

Extreme Positive World: Blue Skies
  • Fonterra's products remain high quality.
  • The NZ dollar weakens = exporting better.
  • High demand + pref. for NZ products = high profits
  • Fonterra continues to hold the monopoly.
  • No "fart tax" is placed on cow emissions.
  • Climte change mitigated by new sequestration tech.

6/8 Add Trends to Worlds

Extreme Negative World: Stormy Days
  • Another Botulism destroys Fonterra’s reputation.
  • The NZ dollar strengthenss = more expensive product.
  • Despite NZ preference, Asian markets seek cheaper alternatives.
  • Fonterra is broken up by NZ government.
  • A high "fart tax" is passed in NZ.
  • Climate change affects production = prices inrease.

7/8 Check Internal Consistency

  • Climate change & low fart tax?
  • NZ preference + increased demand & ability to hold NZ monopoly?

8/8 Add Actions of Actors

Extreme Postive World: Blue Skies
  • Fonterra: Reinvest in distribution and production
  • NZ Government: Tax other areas in dairy production?
Extreme Negative World: Stormy Days
  • Fonterra: Diversify into other markets?
  • Asian Governments: Import restrictions on Fonterra products?
  • NZ Government: Break up Fonterra. Offer dairy subsidies.
