Introduction

What is Uncertainty?

  • Will I graduate?
  • Will nuclear waste storage site experience seismic activity?
  • Will the NZ economy explode?

Saddam Hussein Invades Kuwait

  • Intelligence agencies sent Bush list of predictions.
  • None had first choice that Saddam would attack.
  • Why?
    • Groupthink = group thinking becomes homogeneous.
    • Minority opinions overrun by majority.

Scenario Planning: A Better Way?

  • Build possible scenarios and plan actions.
  • Advantages:
    • Allows minority opinions to have 'airtime'.
    • Predicting future hard; instead, scenarios bound the range of possible outcomes.
    • Participants explore problem space, gaining key insights.

Method 2

The Driving Forces Method

1/3 Outline

  1. Identify issue of concern.
  2. Brainstorm anything that is related to the issue of concern.
  3. Place each issue on impact/predictability quadrant.

2/3 Outline

  1. Focus on issues in bottom right (high impact/low predictability).
  2. Combine the issues in the bottom right into multiple interrelated groups.
  3. Attemp to identify the "driving forces" behind the groups.

3/3 Outline

  1. Pick the top 2-3 driving forces.
  2. Define each driving force by two extremes.
  3. Develop scenarios around the extremes of each driving force. How does the extreme of one combine with the extremes of another?

3/3 Outline

  1. Derive the key implications from the scenarios.
  2. Develop mitigating strategies for negative futures.
  3. Develop exploitative plans for positive futures.

Driving Force Scenario Skeleton

  1. Flesh out the scenarios with issues from the other quadrants.
  2. Develop storylines around the "skeleton" created by the extremes of the driving forces.

Example

  • Concern: The survival and profitability of Fonterra.
  • Identify some issues that relate to this concern:
    1. Global preference for NZ dairy products?
    2. What is Asia's growth rate like?
    3. How does the global uptake of milk products develop?
    4. How do other dairy players in NZ develop?
    5. NZ cow health (disease, outbreaks, etc).
    6. Internal climate within Fonterra.

Example

  • Develop a storyline for each scenario.
  • Example: flourish and fall:
    New Zealand suffers no major epidemics affecting cow health and Fonterra remains the primary co-operative, effectively maintaining their market share as other companies struggle to make ground.
    Although local dominance is maintained, Asia begins to swallow up the global market, reducing the need for New Zealand's dairy export. Coupled with Asia’s dominance, the overall global demand for dairy lowers as the development of cheap milk substitutes picks up speed.
